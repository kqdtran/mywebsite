I was born in Hochiminh City, Vietnam and lived there for the first 18 years of my life. Although I completed the entire K-12 education in my home country, I was fortunate enough to start learning English from 6th grade at Trandainghia High School for the Gifted. 

During my high school years, I was really uncertain about my future, and I couldn't decide what I would like to major in in college. Together with most of my peers, I jumped on the bandwagon and applied to a technical university (University of Science HCM City), and a more business-oriented one (Foreign Trade University). Fortunately, I was admitted to both, but what really made my day was when my parents told me they were going to send me to the U.S. for my higher education and intellectual development.

On September 4th, 2010, a whole new and exciting chapter in my life began as I set foot in the U.S., and started my first semester at Monterey Peninsula College (MPC) only two days later.

In case you are wondering why I chose to go to a community college (CC) and not a four-year university, it was because I was downright lazy in high school. I didn't do thorough research on any particular university or major, nor did I take the SAT test, which is one of the most crucial admission requirements. On a less personal side, going to a CC really helped me and my family financially, while I still got to study a very similar curriculum to most universities in the first two years (at least in the breadth requirements). I didn't regret my decision to go to a community college at all because I got the chance to meet people from all walks of life, some of whom were simply amazing and inspirational. 
During my short two years at MPC, I took various classes required for almost any technical majors, like Calculus, Physics, General Chemistry, and a set of breadth requirements. It was not until I took my very first class in Computer Science (CSIS 10A - Java) did I discover my passion and joy for programming. To be honest, I did learn programming in high school in Pascal because it was required for every student at my school. However, with all due respect, the experience sucked! The way they taught programming in high school for two whole years was just too different to even compare to what I learned in one college class. 

I then enrolled at another college to take an equivalent class to the aforementioned CSIS 10A to learn C++, while at the same time self-taught myself some Python and Javascript. My hard work eventually paid off when I found out that I was admitted to UC Berkeley as an international transfer student for the Fall 2012 Semester. 

I started much later than most people majoring in Computer Science, but my passion and dedication towards CS kept me moving forward. Lying ahead of me are another two years of adventure with new friends, engaging professors, and challenging coursework at the best public institution in the nation. I seriously cannot wait until this August to start a whole new adventure, and I am looking forward to all the crazy, mind-blowing stuff that CS, and Berkeley in general, will throw at me during the next two years.

And as a Cal student, allow me to end my short (or maybe rather long) biography with two words:

GO BEARS!
